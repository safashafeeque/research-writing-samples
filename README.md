# Effects of a Low-Carbohydrate, High-Fat Diet on PCOS Markers in Overweight Women of Reproductive Age

## Overview

This document is a research proposal I completed as part of my research modules in medical school. It explores the possible effects of a low-carbohydrate, high-fat (LCHF) diet on clinical and metabolic markers in overweight women of reproductive age diagnosed with PCOS.

The proposal was developed as an academic research writing project and focuses on the relationship between PCOS, insulin resistance, inflammation, weight, and dietary intervention.

## Research Focus

The main aim of this proposal is to explore whether an LCHF diet could help improve PCOS-related symptoms and markers by supporting weight loss, improving insulin resistance, and reducing inflammation.

## What the Proposal Includes

- Background and rationale for the topic
- Research hypothesis and objectives
- Proposed study design and methodology
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria
- Implementation plan and timeline
- Expected results and limitations
- Ethical considerations

## Why I Chose This Topic

I was interested in this topic because PCOS is a complex condition that affects women in multiple ways, including metabolic, hormonal, reproductive, and psychological health. I wanted to explore whether a dietary approach could offer a more sustainable and meaningful way to support symptom management.

## Document Type

This is an academic proposal and not a completed research study or published paper.
